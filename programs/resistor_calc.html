The Logic Behind Resistor Color Codes

Resistors are too small to have numbers printed on them. Instead, engineers developed a color-coding system to indicate resistance, tolerance, and reliability. This system, established by the IEC 60062 standard, allows you to read values from any angle.

Practical Engineering Insights

When to use 4-Band vs 5-Band?

4-Band (Carbon Film): Common for general use like limiting LED current or Pull-up/down resistors. They are cheaper but have 5% tolerance.

5-Band (Metal Film): Used in precision circuits like Audio Amplifiers or Voltage References. They usually have 1% tolerance (Brown band at the end), ensuring the exact voltage levels required.

What is the 6th Band (PPM)?

PPM stands for Parts Per Million. It indicates how much the resistance changes as the resistor heats up.
Example: A Red (50ppm) band means for every 1¬∞C change in temperature, the resistance shifts by 0.005%. This is critical for military or aerospace electronics where temperature varies wildly.
